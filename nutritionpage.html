Nutrition

We love great tasting food and drinks, created with wellbeing in mind

Whether it’s reducing sugar and salt without compromising on great taste, or offering delicious and innovative plant-based options alongside Costa Coffee classics, we're proud to offer a balanced range of options with the information customers need to choose what’s right for them.

We're working hard to offer a great tasting, high-quality, and balanced food and drink range that is supported by clear and meaningful nutritional information, all helping our customers to make the choice that’s right for them.

Our commitment to your wellbeing

We’re committed to providing credible healthier choices and have a long-term plan to improve the nutritional balance of our menu.

As one part of this commitment, since 2015 we have supported Public Health England’s (PHE) 20% by 2020 sugar reduction programme in food and the 20% by 2021 sugar reduction programme in milk-based drinks.

While our core coffee range contains no added sugar, we have made some significant changes to the rest of the drinks menu through reformulation, introducing more healthier alternatives and reviewing our portion sizes.

Nutrition and allergens guides

Coffee is important to us, but so is the health and wellbeing of our customers. Whether it's checking ingredients for allergens, making your latte lighter, as well as improving our range of vegan offerings, we've got your cravings covered. See our menu for details on our current range of products, or download a full allergen guide below.

Due to ongoing improvements in our food and drink offerings, ingredients may have changed since your last purchase. We can't guarantee that unpackaged items or hand-crafted drinks are safe for those with severe allergies due to shared equipment. You can check our allergen information guides below or speak with a team member at a store for detailed nutritional information.

Working together, to make healthier choices

As a member of the Out of Home Food and Drink Alliance made up of leading High Street brands we have actively engaged with both Department of Health and Public Health England in support of their sugar, salt and calorie reduction programmes as well as initiatives to support clear customer information. Read the Out of Home Food and Drink Alliance’s Code of Practice, here.

We're also a member of the British Nutrition Foundation and work with organisations such as Action on Sugar and Action on Salt to ensure that the decisions we make on nutrition and wellbeing reflect best practice.

  • Our Healthier Choice range

    We understand that while our customers enjoy indulgent treats, they also appreciate healthier options that are equally delicious. That's why we offer our 'Healthier Choice' range of products with controlled levels of salt, sugar, fat, saturated fat, and calories.

    None of these choices are rated 'red' on the Government's nutritional traffic-light colour coding system. Look for the this label to easily find a healthier option when you visit our stores. Quality and taste, without compromising your wellbeing.

  • How to customise your drinks

    * Try a dairy alternative drink. Request a plant based drink, like soya, in your beverage.

    * Go sugar-free. Choose one of our sugar-free syrups without added calories.

    * Downsize your order. Order your favourite drink in a smaller size.

    * Go easy on the syrup. Ask for fewer or no pumps of syrup.

    * Try our light whip. Our vegan alternative to cream is light, tastes amazing and only has 76 kcal per serving.
